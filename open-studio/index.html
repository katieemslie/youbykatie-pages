The creative conundrum

The very thing that makes
you good at your craft is
the thing that makes
running a business exhausting

Nobody talks about this clearly enough. You see meaning and value in everything. You can't do things at 60%. So everything gets 100%. And you don't have 400% to give.

The brand gets 100%

Your own visual identity becomes a personal art project. The website gets rebuilt when it didn't really need to. Beautiful work that nobody sees because you never shipped it.

The admin gets 100%

The invoice template is designed to perfection. The client onboarding process is beautifully crafted. All of it draining the creative energy meant for actual client work.

Then the real work lands

And you're already spent. The projects that actually pay you get whatever energy is left. Which isn't much.

The stuff no one warns you about

The sensitivity to rejection. The fear of putting work out there. The perfectionism that looks like high standards but feels like paralysis. The creeping question: is this sustainable?

You don't need another course. You don't need a guru. You need a room full of people who understand the specific challenge of building a business with a brain that wants to make everything beautiful.

What Open Studio is

A coworking space
for creative brains

Think of it like having access to a creative strategist without them being your boss. A room where you can bring your work, talk through your ideas, sense-check things with other creatives, and borrow Katie's brain for a bit.

Not a course. Not a mastermind. Not a content library you'll never open. A fortnightly space to show up, do the thinking, and leave with clarity.

01

Fortnightly coworking sessions

Two groups of 15, meeting every two weeks. Facilitated by Katie. Part conversation, part structured reflection, part practical strategy. Bring your projects, your half-formed ideas, the things you're stuck on. This is the room where the work gets better.

02

Project sharing and feedback

Show your work. Get honest feedback from creatives who understand the work and the business of it. This includes your own brand, your client projects, your business ideas. A space to sense-check things before you commit. No performance. No grades. Just real input.

03

Borrow Katie's brain

One 30-minute 1:1 session with Katie per quarter, included. Like having a creative strategist in the room who isn't your boss. Strategic feedback, honest perspective, 15 years of creative industry experience at your disposal.

04

A community that actually works

30 members max. Small enough that everyone is seen and heard. Intentionally curated through an application process. Creative professionals who've been doing this long enough to have real context for the conversations. No beginners asking how to find their first client. People in the thick of it.
